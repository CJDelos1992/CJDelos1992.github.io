About

Welcome! I’m a data scientist and researcher with over five years of experience leading high-impact, data-driven projects funded by the U.S. Department of Homeland Security (DHS) and other partners. My work centers on building predictive, causal, and spatio-temporal models that translate complex data into actionable insights for public safety, risk management, and operations.

Drawing on a background in applied mathematics and econometrics, I specialize in connecting rigorous analytical frameworks to real-world decisions. Whether forecasting cybercrime severity across the United States, quantifying insurer loss exposures, or evaluating the effects of emerging technologies such as Ring Neighbors Verified Accounts on property crime, I aim to turn data into strategies that drive measurable impact.

I’m passionate about advancing the responsible use of analytics and technology for public benefit—bridging the gap between statistical theory, policy, and practice. Through interdisciplinary collaboration and a focus on clarity, transparency, and reproducibility, my goal is to produce tools and evidence that empower decision-makers across sectors.
